# Product

## Register

product

## Users

FORZE is primarily for professional construction budgeting teams. The initial primary user is a civil engineer or estimator who creates projects, builds civil-work budgets, manages chapters and rubros, prepares APU, updates quantities, yields, material prices, labor and equipment costs, compares scenarios, and exports a final quote.

Secondary users include construction company managers or owners who review profitability and approve final budget versions; purchasing staff who will later compare suppliers and update input costs; site administrators who may later compare budget against execution; and external clients who should only receive approved documents or limited consultation views.

## Product Purpose

FORZE helps make a complex construction budget feel controllable, verifiable, and fast to build. Its first-version workflow centers on creating a project, registering client/location/target amount, importing or building a budget structure, linking rubros to APU, recovering historical costs, adapting prices by date/location/provider/volume, calculating direct and indirect costs, simulating adjustments, approving a version, generating the client quote, and preserving traceability of every value.

FORZE should not merely distribute a received amount across rubros. It should help determine whether the target amount allows the work to be executed in a viable and profitable way.

## Brand Personality

FORZE should feel sober, technical, operational, clean, modern, and solid. It should project precision, structure, control, confidence, reliability, and economic decision-making power without becoming aggressive or decorative.

The interface should favor organized density, fast navigation, keyboard-oriented workflows, editable tables, persistent filters, bulk actions, traceability, clear calculations, and visible states.

## Anti-references

FORZE must not look like a generic startup landing page, a banking or crypto app, an old ERP, an unstructured spreadsheet clone, an informal social app, or an aggressive industrial brand. Avoid oversized promotional blocks, excessive gradients, abstract illustrations, decorative animations, low-density card layouts, endless forms, deep menu trees, tiny inconsistent buttons, overly saturated colors, cartoonish icons, and excessive rounding.

## Design Principles

1. Make cost origin and validity visible.
2. Optimize for dense daily work, not presentation.
3. Keep calculations and states legible under pressure.
4. Use product-standard interactions before inventing new ones.
5. Communicate strength through structure and precision.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

The product should target accessible professional workflows with visible focus states, keyboard navigation, readable contrast, responsive layouts, and reduced-motion support. Client-facing surfaces must protect confidential internal costs, suppliers, margins, and yields by design.
